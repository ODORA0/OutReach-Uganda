Why Your Sponsorship Matters

The Reality in Kivulu

In the Makerere–Kivulu slum, many children face the harsh reality of food insecurity, lack of sanitation, and limited access to education. Without intervention, the cycle of poverty continues to the next generation.

Our Intervention

Outreach Uganda provides a holistic safety net. We don't just pay school fees; we provide mentorship, daily meals, and a safe place to play. We believe in nurturing the whole child—mind, body, and spirit.

Education Transforms Lives

Our classroom programs provide quality education to over 200 children in Makerere–Kivulu.

Safe Spaces to Play & Grow

Every child deserves a safe place to be a child, away from the dangers of the streets.

Homework Support & Tutoring

We provide after-school programs to help children excel academically.

Meet the Children

Sarah's Journey to Top of the Class

"I want to be a doctor so I can help my community."

Two years ago, Sarah (12) was selling maize on the streets to support her siblings. She had dropped out of school and lost hope. When our team met her, we saw a spark.

Through our scholarship program, Sarah returned to school. Today, she is not only catching up but leading her class. She has become a role model for other girls in Kivulu.

Impact: Full Tuition & Uniform Support

David Finds a Safe Haven

"The center is my safe place. I feel free here."

David (10) used to spend his days wandering the dangerous alleys of the slum while his parents looked for work. He was exposed to drugs and violence at a young age.

Now, David spends his afternoons at our Community Center. He is the captain of our Under-12 soccer team and is receiving counseling to help him process his trauma. He is learning that he is valuable and loved.

Impact: Mentorship & Safe Play Program

Nutrition for Little Moses

"Now I am strong enough to play."

When we first met Moses (6), he was severely malnourished and too weak to run with the other children. His single mother struggled to provide even one meal a day.

Through our daily feeding program, Moses now receives a hot, nutritious lunch every day. The transformation has been physical and emotional—his energy is back, his grades have improved, and his smile lights up the room.

Impact: Daily Feeding Program

Grace's University Dream

"I will be the first in my family to graduate."

Grace (17) is one of our oldest beneficiaries. Growing up in Kivulu, few expected her to make it to high school. But with consistent support, she is now preparing for her national exams.

She volunteers on weekends to tutor younger children in the program, giving back the same hope she received.

Impact: High School Scholarship & Leadership Training
